# Introduction

A Python/Matplotlib project that visualizes arithmetic, figurate, recursive, and digit-based classes of integers on a \(10 \times 10\) grid containing the numbers from 1 to 100. Each category is represented by colored cells over a black background. During the transition from one category to the next, the highlighted squares move continuously across the grid rather than simply disappearing and reappearing. The result is suitable for educational videos, YouTube Shorts, Instagram Reels, TikTok, lectures, and mathematical outreach.


# Included number categories

The default animation contains the following 34 categories.

| # | Category | Definition used in the project |
|---:|---|---|
| 1 | Natural Numbers | All integers from 1 to 100 |
| 2 | Odd Numbers | Integers not divisible by 2 |
| 3 | Even Numbers | Integers divisible by 2 |
| 4 | Multiples of 3 | Integers divisible by 3 |
| 5 | Multiples of 5 | Integers divisible by 5 |
| 6 | Multiples of 7 | Integers divisible by 7 |
| 7 | Multiples of 10 | Integers divisible by 10 |
| 8 | Prime Numbers | Integers greater than 1 with exactly two positive divisors |
| 9 | Composite Numbers | Integers greater than 1 that are not prime |
| 10 | Semiprime Numbers | Products of exactly two prime factors, counted with multiplicity |
| 11 | Square Numbers | Integers of the form \(n^2\) |
| 12 | Cube Numbers | Integers of the form \(n^3\) |
| 13 | Perfect Powers | Integers of the form \(a^b\), with \(a \ge 2\) and \(b \ge 2\) |
| 14 | Triangular Numbers | \(T_n = n(n+1)/2\) |
| 15 | Tetrahedral Numbers | \(Te_n = n(n+1)(n+2)/6\) |
| 16 | Square Pyramidal Numbers | \(P_n = n(n+1)(2n+1)/6\) |
| 17 | Pentagonal Numbers | \(P_n = n(3n-1)/2\) |
| 18 | Hexagonal Numbers | \(H_n = n(2n-1)\) |
| 19 | Perfect Numbers | Equal to the sum of their proper positive divisors |
| 20 | Abundant Numbers | Smaller than the sum of their proper divisors |
| 21 | Deficient Numbers | Greater than the sum of their proper divisors |
| 22 | Fibonacci Numbers | Terms of the Fibonacci sequence not exceeding 100 |
| 23 | Decimal Harshad Numbers | Divisible by the sum of their decimal digits |
| 24 | Sphenic Numbers | Products of three distinct prime numbers |
| 25 | Smith Numbers | Composite numbers whose digit sum equals the digit sum of their prime factors |
| 26 | Binary Palindromic Numbers | Binary expansion is a palindrome |
| 27 | Decimal Palindromic Numbers | Decimal representation is a palindrome |
| 28 | Happy Numbers | Repeated sum-of-squares-of-digits iteration reaches 1 |
| 29 | Lucky Numbers | Generated by the classical lucky-number sieve |
| 30 | Evil Numbers | Binary expansion contains an even number of ones |
| 31 | Automorphic Numbers | Their square ends with the original number |
| 32 | Kaprekar Numbers | Their square can be split into two parts whose sum is the original number |
| 33 | Catalan Numbers | Terms of the Catalan sequence not exceeding 100 |
| 34 | Pell Numbers | Terms of the Pell sequence not exceeding 100 |
